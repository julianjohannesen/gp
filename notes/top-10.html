Part 1: Voter Registration and Voting

Make it easier to register and vote for all citizens.

  1. Same Day Voter Registration

    Allow voters to register to vote or fix a problem with their registration (e.g. address change, name change) when they vote.

  2. Automatic Voter Registration

    Register people to vote or update their registration at motor vehicles and other government agencies.

  3. Early Voting

    Expand convenient options to vote early by mail, or to drop off a completed ballot at a Drop Box or a poll on Election Day.

    Every state should have robust options to vote by mail, early-in person or on Election Day. The most effective is to send every registered voter a mail ballot 2-3 weeks before the election with convenient options to return the ballot by mail, drop box, or at vote center on Election Day. See how it works in Colorado

  4. Voting Rights Restoration

    Restore the vote for all people released from prison, including those on probation and parole. Ensure all 50 states have the same standard for who is eligible to vote in state and federal elections. (Withholding the vote post-prison serves no public purpose and only undercuts rehabilitation and better citizenship.)

  5. Non-Partisan Election Administration

    Have a non-partisan and professional process to oversee voting and count the votes.

Part 2: Election Methods - How We Vote

Voting methods that encourage political competition, provide a level playing field, and give all voters meaningful choices when they cast their ballot

  1. Ranked Choice Voting

    Ensure election winners have majority support. Give voters more choice. Allow more than two candidates or parties to run without fear of splitting the vote.

  2. Non-Partisan and Fair Redistricting

    All states create non-partisan redistricting commissions and other methods to establish fair redistricting criteria and to safeguard against racial or partisan gerrymandering.

  3. Open Primaries

    Allow independent voters to vote in the party primary of their choice. Or establish a single non-partisan primary that, like Alaska, qualifies more than two candidates to compete in the general election.
